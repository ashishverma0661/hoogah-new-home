Our Belief — Act 1 of 5

Networking is why people come to events.

People don’t just attend events for content.

They come to —

  • meet new people.
  • exchange ideas.
  • learn from others.
  • have conversations they wouldn’t have otherwise.

IV
Our Observation

Everything else at events is designed. Networking is the one thing we hope works out.

Sessions are planned, speakers are curated, agendas are structured to the minute. The one moment that actually determines whether an event is worth coming back to is the one moment nobody plans for.

Curated. Planned. Structured.

Sessions, speakers, agendas — every minute of the event is intentional and accounted for.

Open time. A reception. A room.

Networking is left to whoever’s already comfortable starting conversations — and the assumption it will just happen.

Act 5 of 5

Connection should be designed.

Not left to chance.

Not dependent on who’s most comfortable starting conversations.

When people know —

  • who they’re meeting.
  • why they were matched.

Better conversations happen naturally.
