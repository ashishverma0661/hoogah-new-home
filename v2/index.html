Our Belief
01

Networking is why people come to events.

They come to meet new people, learn from others, and have conversations they wouldn't have otherwise.

02

But it doesn't always happen.

People scan the room and stick with who they already know. Most attendees miss the conversations that matter.

03

What gets missed.

The people worth meeting most aren't always the ones you naturally end up talking to. That's a design problem.

04

How most events handle networking.

Sessions are planned. Speakers are curated. But networking is left to open time, and the assumption it will just happen.

Connection should be designed.
Not left to chance.

That’s why we built Hoogah. To make it easy for the right people to meet — without forcing interactions, without adding complexity.

Use Cases

From executive summits to sales kickoffs, Hoogah helps attendees move beyond surface-level networking, so real conversations actually happen.

01

Executive Summits

What usually happens

Conversations stay high-level. People don't always get into the challenges they're actually facing.

What changes

Leaders are paired with peers working through similar situations.

Why it matters

02

User Conferences

What usually happens

Attendees learn from sessions, but rarely exchange ideas with others doing similar work.

What changes

Customers are introduced to peers facing similar problems or stages.

Why it matters

03

Sales Kickoffs (SKOs)

What usually happens

Energy is high, but conversations don't extend beyond immediate teams.

What changes

Reps are connected across regions, roles, and levels.

Why it matters

More cross-team alignment and idea exchange going into the year.

04

Corporate Events & Town Halls

What usually happens

Employees attend, but interaction stays surface-level or within existing groups.

What changes

Why it matters

Stronger cross-functional relationships, improving idea exchange and culture.
